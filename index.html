Career and Research

September 1938 - Cryptanalyst
- Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS) - cryptanalysed the Enigma cipher machine of Nazi Germany and developed bombe to decode its messages, deduced the indicator procedure used by the German Navy, developed a statistical procedure Banburismus for more efficient use of the bombe, developed Turingery for working out the cam settings of the wheels of the Lorenz SZ 40/42 (Tunny) cipher machine and developed a portable source of secure voice scrambler (Delilah).

1942 - Cryptanalyst
- worked with the US Navy cryptanalysts on the naval Enigma and bombe construction in Washington, visited their Computing Machine Laboratory in Dayton, Ohio, assisted Bell Labs with the development of secure speech devices.
1945-1947 - Computer Scientist
- worked on the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE) at the National Physical Laboratory (NPL)

19 February 1946 - Computer Scientist
- presented a paper on the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.

1947 - Logician
- researched and produced work on Intelligent Machinery

1948 - Reader
- Mathematics Department at the Victoria University of Manchester.

1949 - Deputy Director
- Computing Machine Laboratory and worked on the software
for the Manchester Mark 1.

1949 - Computer Scientist
- Recruited for development of the commercialised machine, 'Ferranti Mark 1'.

1950 - Mathematician
- abstract work in mathematics

1950 - Logician
- "Computing Machinery and Intelligence".

1950 - Logician
- proposed the 'Turing test' to test the intelligence of machines.

January 1952 - Theoretical Biologist
- published "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" suggesting the reason for the phenomena of morphogenesis.
